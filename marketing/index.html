How it teaches

Four ways to learn.

One conversation, four pedagogical postures. Pick the one that fits what you're trying to do.

Socratic

The default. Mercurius asks questions back instead of handing you the answer. Use this when you want to learn how to think through a problem, not just collect a result.

Try: "How does an LLM actually work?"

Direct

Plain, efficient answers — no Socratic detours. Unlocks after you demonstrate critical thinking in Socratic Mode, so the depth feels earned rather than handed over.

Try: "Give me the formula for exponential decay."

Debate

You take a position; Mercurius pushes the other side. Responses come structured as claim, warrant, impact, and rebuttal angle — punchy enough for speech prep or live cross-examination.

Try: "Argue against the claim that AI will replace teachers."

Discussion

Conversational and balanced. Mercurius treats you as a thinking partner — surfacing tradeoffs and tensions, not verdicts.

Try: "What perspectives matter most when thinking about AI bias?"

Responsibility

Built for responsible learning.

We don't promise the future of AI is risk-free. We do promise the tutor is honest about what it is.

  • Learning-first design. Won't write your homework. Will help you understand how to think about it.
  • Calibrated honesty. Every substantive reply carries a confidence signal — high, medium, low, "I'm not sure."
  • Bias awareness. Mercurius is upfront about the limits of its training data and the perspectives it might be missing.
  • Academic integrity. Asking for an essay redirects to the thinking behind it.
  • Privacy-conscious. No personal data required. Local chat history stays on your device.
